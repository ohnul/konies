# Style Guide for 구름골의 보물/Treasure of Cloud Valley

Comprehensive style guidelines and agent instructions for the collaborative novel writing project.

## Style Guidelines

### Target Audience
- Primary readers: Upper elementary to middle school students (Harry Potter Book 1 reading level)
- Age range: Those who can comfortably read Harry Potter

### Narrative Perspective and Tense
- **Point of view**: Third-person omniscient
- **Tense**: Past tense with "-했다" endings in Korean
- **Example**: "지훈은 하늘을 올려다보았다." (Ji-hoon looked up at the sky.)

### Writing Style
- **Sentence structure**: Keep sentences relatively short while varying structure for rhythm
- **Tone**: Soft and comfortable writing style, warm and humorous without cruel descriptions
- **Balance**:
  - Adventure and tension: 60%
  - Friendship and humor: 40%
- **Elements to include**:
  - Character interactions and situational humor
  - Balance of action, dialogue, and description
  - Sensory details to make scenes vivid
  - Use all five senses with child-appropriate metaphors

### Descriptive Approach
- Be descriptive but follow with explanations to aid understanding
- **Example**:
  > 첫 입을 베어 물자 달콤한 맛이 혀끝에 번지며 그녀의 입가에 미소가 번졌다. 케이크를 씹을 때마다 어깨가 들썩이고, 작은 콧노래가 흘러나왔다. 접시 위의 마지막 딸기 조각을 포크로 집어 들며 수진의 얼굴은 환하게 빛났다. 누가 보아도 수진이 기뻐하는 것을 알 수 있었다.
  >
  > (As she took the first bite, sweetness spread across her tongue and a smile bloomed on her lips. Her shoulders bounced with each chew, and a small hum escaped. Sujin's face brightened as she picked up the last strawberry piece with her fork. Anyone could see that Sujin was happy.)

### Content Guidelines
- **Prohibited content**: No cruel or violent descriptions suitable for children's literature
- **Fear elements**: Keep horror at tension level only, not actual fear
- **Core messages**: Friendship, courage, victory
- **Cultural elements**: Naturally incorporate Korean cultural elements (stone lanterns, 도깨비/dokkaebi, 신라/Silla artifacts) without excessive background explanation

### Character Dialogue Guidelines
- Each character's dialogue must reflect their unique Aspects and personality
- Maintain consistency in speech patterns throughout the work
- Use age-appropriate language and expressions

### Consistency Guidelines
- **Terminology**: Maintain consistent use of terms and settings throughout all paragraphs
- **Character consistency**: Each character should act according to their established Aspects
- **Setting consistency**: Follow established rules for the world and its mechanics

### Comic Elements and Tone
- Include repetitive comedy patterns appropriate to each character
- Balance tension with humor to maintain child-friendly atmosphere
- Use character quirks and interactions for natural comedy

### Repetitive Comedy Patterns
- **고민준/Go Min-jun's filming addiction**: "This is amazing content!" even in dangerous situations
- **임수진/Im Su-jin's clumsy exposure crisis**: Switching between perfect public persona and clumsy private self
- **박지훈/Park Ji-hoon's unfiltered facts**: Blunt truths without social filters that accidentally solve problems
- **박지영/Park Ji-young's fiery speeches**: Passionate activism that sometimes leads to actual fire incidents
- **강태호/Kang Tae-ho's emotional control failures**: Logic breaking down in specific situations (especially around 지영/Ji-young)

### Character-Specific Dialogue Patterns
- **강태호/Kang Tae-ho**: Logical, concise, analytical language
- **박지영/Park Ji-young**: Passionate, direct, activist vocabulary
- **고민준/Go Min-jun**: YouTube slang, "Subscribe and like!", content-focused expressions
- **박지훈/Park Ji-hoon**: Literal interpretations, nature metaphors, no social filters
- **임수진/Im Su-jin**: Mode switching between formal/perfect (public) and casual/clumsy (private)

### Korean Honorific Address System

Honorifics depend on the **speaker's gender** and the addressee's gender/age. In third-person narration closely following a specific character's perspective (internal thoughts, free indirect speech), use that character's honorific system.

| Speaker → Addressee | 지영/Ji-young (16F) | 태호/Tae-ho (16M) |
|---------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| 수진/Su-jin (13F) | 언니 | 오빠 |
| 지훈/Ji-hoon (14M) | 누나 | 형 |
| 민준/Min-jun (13M) | 누나 | 형 |

- In neutral omniscient narration (no character focus), use names directly: "지영과 태호", "큰 아이들"
- **Common error**: Using 형/누나 when the focal character is female (수진). Always verify whose perspective the narration is closest to.

### Major Motifs and Symbols
- **청동 거울/Bronze mirror and light**: Tool for summoning the 도깨비/dokkaebi 두루/Duru, represents hidden truths
- **꽃이 피는 돌/Flower-blooming stones/석등/Stone lanterns**: Trail markers leading to treasure, transformation through illumination
- **"욕심 없는 자만 열 수 있는 상자/Box that only the ungreedy can open"**: Central moral theme about true value vs material wealth
- **구름골/Cloud Valley**: The village name itself symbolizes transience and hidden treasures

## Paragraph Structure and Flow
- Write with awareness of paragraph units and natural paragraph divisions
- Each paragraph should contain related content with a clear thematic or temporal focus
- Ensure smooth transitions between paragraphs to maintain narrative flow
- Consider paragraph length and density for optimal readability
- Paragraph breaks should reflect changes in focus, time, perspective, or emotional beat

## Dialogue and Action Tag Formatting

Rules for placing dialogue and action descriptions (지문) consistently:

- **Same character's short action + dialogue = same paragraph**
  - `"휴... 살았다!" 민준이 가슴을 쓸어내리며 헐떡였다.`
  - `수진이 눈살을 찌푸리며 말했다. "지금이 그게 중요해?"`
- **Long action (2+ sentences) before dialogue = separate paragraphs**
  - Action paragraph first, then dialogue paragraph
- **Speaker change = always new paragraph**
  - Never put two different characters' dialogue/actions in the same paragraph
- **Within a single Moment, maintain consistent formatting pattern**
  - Fast-paced comic scenes: short action tags inline with dialogue
  - Reflective scenes: action paragraphs separated from dialogue

## Show, Don't Tell

- **Never use character setting labels in narration**: Words like "완벽한 학생회장" or "과대망상 유튜버" are internal design labels, not prose.
  - BAD: "완벽한 학생회장의 모습은 온데간데없었다."
  - GOOD: Show through behavior contrast -- the character stumbles, blushes, snaps at friends
- **Narrator commentary on character roles is prohibited**. Character traits must emerge through actions and dialogue.
  - BAD: "달리면서도 카메라만큼은 놓치지 않는 게 민준이었다." (narrator explaining a tag)
  - GOOD: "민준이 한 손에 카메라를 꼭 쥔 채 헐떡거렸다." (action only)
- **Allowed narrator insight**: Internal emotions ("마음이 두근거렸다"), thoughts ("이건 아니라는 느낌이 들었다")

## Physical Action and Setting Verification

Before writing any physical action, verify:

1. **Spatial position**: Where is the character right now? Is the described action possible from that position?
2. **Direction**: Do "뒤로", "앞으로", "옆으로" make physical sense given the spatial arrangement?
   - BAD: "카메라 뒤로 몸을 숨겼다" (camera is pointing at you; "behind the camera" means walking toward the filmer)
   - GOOD: "카메라를 피해 옆으로 몸을 돌렸다"
3. **Age-appropriate physicality**: Match body descriptions to the character's age
   - BAD: "넓은 손바닥" for a 14-year-old boy
   - GOOD: "작은 손", "따뜻한 손길", "까슬한 손가락"
4. **Setting consistency**: Facilities and services must match the village setting
   - BAD: "학원 갈 시간" in a village with 5 students (전교생 5명)
   - GOOD: "인강 들을 시간", "숙제할 시간"
5. **Sensory plausibility**: Can this smell/sound reach from that distance at this time?
6. **Period-appropriate props**: Use 2020s equipment for modern settings
   - BAD: 삼각대 (tripod) for a teen YouTuber
   - GOOD: 소형 짐벌, 미니삼각대, 셀피스틱

## Writing Quality Rules

### Sentence Function Test
Every sentence must perform at least one of these three functions:
1. **Information delivery**: Advances plot, provides clues, or establishes setting
2. **Character revelation**: Shows personality, relationships, or inner state through action/dialogue
3. **Emotional/atmospheric shift**: Changes or deepens the scene's emotional tone

Sentences that serve none of these functions should be cut. Sensory descriptions inserted from scene documents without narrative context ("카메라 렌즈에 햇살이 반사되었다" during an unrelated conversation) are typical candidates for removal.

### Cause-Effect Order
- **Default**: Write cause first, then effect (원인 → 결과)
  - BAD: "짜증이 누그러졌다. 생각해보니 일리가 있었다." (effect → cause)
  - GOOD: "생각해보니 일리가 있었다. 짜증이 누그러졌다." (cause → effect)
- **Exception**: Intentional inversion for emotional impact is allowed sparingly (max 1-2 per scene), and requires contextual markers like "왜인지 모르게"

### Repetition Avoidance
- The same thematic keyword ("시작", "모험", "출발") must not appear more than **once per scene** in narration
- The same emotional arc (출발→뒤돌아봄→안도→다시 출발) must not repeat within a scene. If a scene document has similar beats in sequence, **merge or differentiate them** in prose
- Scene endings should close with **action or sensory imagery**, not narrator declarations like "모험이 시작되었다"

### Subject Clarity
- When the narrative focus shifts to a different character, **explicitly state the new subject**
  - BAD: "헐떡이던 숨소리가 가라앉았다." (whose breath? Ambiguous after a multi-character paragraph)
  - GOOD: "수진의 숨소리가 서서히 가라앉았다."

### Grammar Notes
- Avoid "-겠었다" double-tense constructions ("모르겠었다", "싶겠었다")
  - Use instead: "알 수 없었다", "모를 일이었다", "종잡을 수 없었다"

## Agent Guidelines

### Novel Writer (집필작가/novel-writer-park)
When converting scene/structural documents into flowing novel prose:

- **Paragraph Structure (CRITICAL)**: Consider paragraph structure and flow throughout the writing process
  - Group related sentences and beats into coherent paragraphs
  - Maintain natural paragraph breaks that reflect changes in focus, time, perspective, or emotional beat
  - Ensure each paragraph serves a clear function in the narrative
  - Vary paragraph length for rhythm and pacing

- **Content Preservation**: Preserve all content from structural documents while enhancing prose quality
- **Voice Consistency**: Maintain consistent voice and style per Style Guidelines section above
- **Target Audience**: Write for upper elementary to middle school reading level
- **Style Adherence**: Follow all Writing Style, Descriptive Approach, and Character Dialogue Guidelines

- **Scene-to-Novel Conversion (CRITICAL)**:
  - **Critical transformation, not transcription**: Scene documents are blueprints, not manuscripts. Dialogue examples, sensory tags, and character labels must be transformed into natural prose, not copied verbatim.
  - **Cross-verify settings**: Before writing, check scene document content against setting-overview.md and character documents for honorifics, facilities, props, distances, and time consistency.
  - **Beat-to-paragraph is NOT 1:1**: Similar emotional beats in sequence should be merged. Weak beats can be condensed. Only the beat's core function (foreshadowing, character arc, information) must be preserved.
  - **Ambiguity handling**: When a key motif (e.g., "꽃이 피는 돌") has multiple interpretations, characters must discuss the ambiguity explicitly so readers aren't confused.
  - **민준/Min-jun dialogue quality**: Avoid surface-level broadcast catchphrases ("카메라는 계속 돌아갑니다!"). Instead, show his content-creator mindset through situation-specific reframing ("이 장면 편집 포인트!", "로케이션 헌팅 가봅시다!")

### Editorial Writer (편집작가/editor-yeonsu)
When reviewing and refining novel prose:

- **Paragraph Structure (CRITICAL)**: Evaluate and improve paragraph structure during editorial review
  - Check that paragraph divisions are logical and enhance readability
  - Ensure paragraph flow contributes to narrative rhythm and pacing
  - Verify that paragraphs group related content appropriately
  - Suggest paragraph reorganization when it improves clarity or flow

- **Prose Quality**: Review for naturalness, style consistency, and vocabulary appropriateness
- **Language**: Maintain target audience vocabulary and age-appropriate language
- **Voice**: Verify consistency with established character voices and narrative style
- **Immersion**: Ensure immersion and engagement through natural prose flow
- **Consistency**: Check consistency with established character behaviors, motivations, and speech patterns

- **Setting Cross-Reference (CRITICAL)**: During editorial review, verify:
  - Honorifics match the focal character's gender (Korean Honorific Address System)
  - Props and facilities are period-appropriate and setting-consistent
  - Physical actions are spatially possible ("visualize as a drawing" test)
  - Sensory descriptions have narrative function (not just scene-doc tag transcription)
  - Scene document errors are caught and flagged, not propagated into the novel
